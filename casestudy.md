# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

---

## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into sending money or sharing sensitive payment details through deception. It is one of the most rapidly growing cybercrimes in India, affecting people of all ages, including college students.

---

## How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

**Scenario: The "Wrong Transfer" Scam**

1. A student receives a call or WhatsApp message from an unknown number. The caller claims they have accidentally transferred money to the student's UPI ID and requests it back immediately.

2. The caller sounds very polite and stressed, creating a sense of urgency and sympathy.

3. To "return" the money, the fraudster sends a **collect request** via a UPI app. The student, thinking they are simply sending back money, enters their **UPI PIN** to approve the request.

4. However, a collect request is actually a **payment request** – not a receipt. By entering the PIN, the student sends money from their own account to the fraudster.

5. The fraudster immediately blocks the student on all platforms.

---

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time UPI users and young students
- People who sell items on OLX or other online marketplaces
- Anyone who recently posted their contact number publicly

---

## Consequences

- Direct financial loss (often ranging from ₹500 to several thousand rupees)
- Emotional distress and embarrassment
- In some cases, additional phishing attempts using the same contact

---

# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students

---

## 8 Actionable Tips to Protect Yourself from Cybercrime

---

**1. Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone**  
No bank, payment app, or government agency will ever ask for your PIN or OTP over a call, message, or email. If someone asks, it is a scam — hang up immediately.

**2. A "Collect Request" is NOT a credit – it is a debit**  
When you approve a collect/receive request on your UPI app and enter your PIN, you are sending money OUT of your account, not receiving it. Always read the transaction screen carefully before entering your PIN.

**3. Verify unknown callers before acting**  
If someone claims to be from your bank or a company, end the call and call the official customer care number listed on the bank's website. Never call back on the number they provide.

**4. Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts**  
Avoid using the same password across platforms. Use a mix of upper and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Change passwords regularly.

**5. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible**  
Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, and banking apps. This adds an extra layer of protection even if your password is compromised.

**6. Do not click on unknown links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email**  
Fake links often look almost identical to real ones (e.g., sbi-bank-login.com instead of onlinesbi.com). If in doubt, go directly to the official website by typing the URL.

**7. 💳 Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your banking app**  
Most banks allow you to set a daily limit on UPI transfers. Keeping this limit low reduces the potential damage if fraud does occur.

**8. 💳 Register only your primary SIM with your bank account**  
Avoid using your number on public platforms for financial accounts. Use a separate number or email for marketplace listings to reduce exposure.

## How to Report Cybercrime in India

| Resource | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (Available 24x7, all days) |
| **What to report** | UPI fraud, phishing, identity theft, cyberbullying, online harassment, and more |

> If you are a victim of financial fraud, call **1930 immediately**. The sooner you report, the higher the chance of blocking the fraudulent transaction.
